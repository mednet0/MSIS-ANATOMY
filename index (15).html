Anatomy Quiz: Clavicle & Scapula

1. The clavicle is the first bone to ossify in the fetus. At what age does its medial epiphysis fuse?
A) 12–15 years
B) 18–20 years
C) 22–25 years
D) Never fuses
2. A 20-year-old falls on his outstretched hand (FOOSH injury) and presents with shoulder sagging and tenderness over the clavicle. Which part is most likely fractured?
A) Medial end
B) Lateral end
C) Junction of medial 2/3 and lateral 1/3
D) Acromial end
3. Which of the following structures is at risk in a displaced midshaft clavicle fracture?
A) Radial nerve
B) Subclavian vein
C) Femoral artery
D) Sciatic nerve
4. The coracoid process of the scapula serves as an attachment site for all EXCEPT:
A) Pectoralis minor
B) Short head of biceps brachii
C) Supraspinatus
D) Coracobrachialis
5. A patient has difficulty raising his arm above 90° and exhibits "winging" of the scapula when pushing against a wall. Which nerve is most likely injured?
A) Axillary nerve
B) Long thoracic nerve
C) Suprascapular nerve
D) Radial nerve
6. Which rotator cuff muscle originates from the supraspinous fossa of the scapula?
A) Infraspinatus
B) Supraspinatus
C) Teres minor
D) Subscapularis
7. A motorcyclist crashes and sustains a scapular fracture. Which associated injury is most concerning?
A) Clavicle fracture
B) Pulmonary contusion or rib fractures
C) Femur fracture
D) Carpal tunnel syndrome
8. The acromion process of the scapula articulates with which bone?
A) Humerus
B) Clavicle
C) Sternum
D) First rib
9. Which ligament stabilizes the acromioclavicular (AC) joint?
A) Coracoacromial ligament
B) Coracoclavicular ligament
C) Transverse humeral ligament
D) Annular ligament
10. A patient with a history of repetitive overhead throwing presents with shoulder pain and weakness in external rotation. Which structure is most likely affected?
A) Clavicle
B) Infraspinatus muscle (suprascapular nerve)
C) Pectoralis major
D) Biceps brachii
